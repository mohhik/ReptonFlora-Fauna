Olive Olea europaea L. Mediterranean climates (hot/dry summers, mild/wet winters), grasslands, shrublands. Mediterranean region (Southern Europe, Northern Africa, Western Asia).
Weeping Fig Ficus benjamina L. Primary/Secondary Rainforest, Monsoon Forest. Indian subcontinent, Southern China, Southeast Asia, Northern Australia, Southwestern Pacific.
Date Palm Phoenix dactylifera L. Terrestrial (Desert / Semi-Desert) oases. Uncertain due to ancient cultivation; generally accepted as Fertile Crescent/Persian Gulf region.
Kikuyu Grass Cenchrus clandestinus (Hochst. ex Chiov.) Morrone Seasonally dry tropical biome, favors moist areas, grasslands. East African highlands (Eritrea, Ethiopia, Kenya, Tanzania, etc.).
St. Augustine Grass Stenotaphrum secundatum (Walter) Kuntze Coastal/wetland areas (sandy beaches, swamps, lagoons, marshes, streams). Southeastern U.S.A., South America, and Africa.
Mexican Petunia Ruellia simplex C. Wright Marshy/wet areas, ditches, pond shores; also tolerates drier sites. Southern North America, Southern South America, and the Antilles.
Small Leaf Clusia Clusia fluminensis Planch. & Triana Littoral forests, often very close to the sea in sandy soils. Eastern Brazil (South America).
Panicum Preontis Coleataenia prionitis (Nees) Soreng Tropical and subtropical grasslands and disturbed areas. Native to South America, specifically Brazil and neighboring regions.
Snowbush Breynia disticha J.R.Forst. & G.Forst. Widely cultivated in tropical locales; native habitat is the western Pacific. Native to New Caledonia and Vanuatu in the western Pacific.
Pokosola / Bloodhorn Ochrosia elliptica Labill. Grows naturally in north-eastern Australia and the southwest Pacific. Native to north-eastern Australia and the southwest Pacific.
Lesser Bougainvillea Bougainvillea glabra Choisy Grows in well-drained sandy desert soils, slopes, and disturbed rocky soil. Native to Brazil.
Tamarind Tamarindus indica L. Dry savannas, woodlands, and deciduous bushlands. Native to tropical Africa.
Japanese Yew Taxus cuspidata Siebold & Zucc. Evergreen forests and temperate regions. Native to Japan, Korea, northeast China, and the extreme southeast of Russia.
Mareer Cordia subcordata Lam. Seasonally dry tropical biome, especially coastal forests. Somalia to Mozambique, across the western Indian Ocean to the Pacific islands.
Geigertree Cordia sebestena L. Coastal environments, dry broadleaf evergreen forests. American tropics (Southern Florida, Bahamas, Central America, Greater Antilles).
White Henbane Hyoscyamus albus L. Dry, uncultivated ground, walls, and field margins, often near settlements in the Mediterranean region. Southern Europe, North Africa, West Asia, and Macaronesia.

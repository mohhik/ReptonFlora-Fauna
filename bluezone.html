Senior School - Blue Zone

Number of Species: 22

Habitat Type: Diverse (Dry Savannas, Coastal, Mediterranean, Rainforest)

SPECIES (COMMON NAME) SCIENTIFIC NAME HABITAT TYPE COMMONLY FOUND AREAS (NATIVE RANGE)
Tamarind Tamarindus indica L. Dry savannas, woodlands, and deciduous bushlands. Native to tropical Africa. Widely cultivated globally in tropical and subtropical regions.
Japanese Yew Taxus cuspidata Siebold & Zucc. Evergreen forests and temperate regions. Native to Japan, Korea, northeast China, and the extreme southeast of Russia.
Mareer Cordia subcordata Lam. Seasonally dry tropical biome, especially coastal forests. Somalia to Mozambique, across the western Indian Ocean to the Pacific islands.
Geigertree Cordia sebestena L. Coastal environments, dry broadleaf evergreen forests. American tropics (Southern Florida, Bahamas, Central America, Greater Antilles).
Bermudagrass Cynodon dactylon (L.) Pers. Grasslands, savannas, and disturbed sites. Native to the Old World tropics and subtropics (Europe, Africa, Asia, and Australia).
Yellow Trumpet Handroanthus ochraceus (Cham.) Mattos Wet tropical biome, including forests, savannas, and shrublands. Central America to northwestern Argentina and Trinidad.
Common Boxwood Buxus sempervirens L. Understory of forests, often on chalk/limestone soils. Western and southern Europe, northwest Africa, and southwest Asia.
Mexican Petunia Ruellia simplex C. Wright Marshy/wet areas, wetlands, and disturbed sites. Southern North America, Southern South America, and the Antilles.
Olive Olea europaea L. Mediterranean climates, grasslands, shrublands. Mediterranean region (Southern Europe, Northern Africa, Western Asia).
Date Palm Phoenix dactylifera L. Terrestrial (Desert / Semi-Desert) oases. Uncertain, generally accepted as Fertile Crescent/Persian Gulf region.
Weeping Fig Ficus benjamina L. Primary/Secondary Rainforest, Monsoon Forest. Indian subcontinent, Southern China, Southeast Asia, Northern Australia.
Kikuyu Grass Cenchrus clandestinus (Hochst. ex Chiov.) Morrone Seasonally dry tropical biome, favors moist areas, grasslands. East African highlands (Eritrea, Ethiopia, Kenya, Tanzania, etc.).
St. Augustine Grass Stenotaphrum secundatum (Walter) Kuntze Coastal/wetland areas (sandy beaches, swamps, lagoons, marshes). Southeastern U.S.A., South America, and Africa.
Frangipani Plumeria obtusa L. Shrublands and coppices, often on rocky or sandy substrates. West Indies, southern Mexico, and Central America.
Florida Hopbush Dodonaea viscosa Jacq. Coastal areas (dunes, dry hammocks) and inland scrub. Widespread globally in tropical, subtropical, and warm temperate regions.
Coco Plum Chrysobalanus icaco L. Coastal areas (beaches, dunes, scrubs) and inland swamps/riverbanks. Tropical Africa, tropical Americas and the Caribbean, including southern Florida.
Ziricote Cordia dodecandra A.DC. Dry tropical forests and thickets, often in calcareous soils. Central Mexico to Guatemala, Belize, Honduras, and Cuba.
Peregrina Jatropha integerrima Jacq. Sandy and semi-arid areas, close to swampy zones. West Indies, including Cuba and Hispaniola.
Bottlebrush Callistemon (Genus) Moist conditions, often along creek beds (temperate to tropical regions). Endemic to Australia and New Caledonia.
Japanese Box Buxus microphylla Siebold & Zucc. Not known in the truly wild, widely cultivated in temperate regions. Native to Eastern Asia (Korea, China, Vietnam); long cultivated in Japan and Taiwan.
Small Leaf Clusia Clusia fluminensis Planch. & Triana Littoral forests, close to the sea in sandy soils. Eastern Brazil (South America).
Bottlebrush Coleataenia prionitis (Nees) Soreng Tropical and subtropical grasslands and disturbed areas. Native to South America, specifically Brazil and neighboring regions.

Carpark - Green Zone

Number of Species: 12

Habitat Type: Subtropical, Coastal/Littoral, Evergreen Forest

SPECIES (COMMON NAME) SCIENTIFIC NAME HABITAT TYPE COMMONLY FOUND AREAS (NATIVE RANGE)
Golden Dew Drops Duranta erecta L. Evergreen sprawling shrub found in rocky or sandy coastal areas, or moister, disturbed sites inland. Native to the Americas, from Mexico and the Caribbean south to Brazil and Argentina.
Henna Lawsonia inermis L. Shrub or tree growing primarily in the subtropical biome. Found in dry, coastal scrublands. Native range is Northeast Tropical Africa, the Arabian Peninsula, and South Pakistan to India.
Sweet Mock Orange Philadelphus coronarius L. Scrub and woodland on warm rocky hills and screes. Native to the Caucasus and northeastern Turkey, and parts of Southern Europe (e.g., S. Austria, Italy).
Zoysia / Manila Grass Zoysia matrella (L.) Merr. Perennial grass growing primarily in the wet tropical biome. Grows in low elevations, preferring sandy soils. Native to Tropical & Subtropical Asia to the Northwest Pacific, including Southern Japan, Taiwan, China, Southeast Asia, and Northern Australia.
Mamey Sapote Pouteria sapota (Jacq.) H.E.Moore & Stearn Prefers smooth sandy, deep, clay and fertile soils; does not tolerate low temperatures or prolonged drought. Southern Mexico and Central America; likely from southern Mexico (Veracruz, Tabasco, Chiapas) through Nicaragua, Belize, and northern Honduras.
Japanese Yew Taxus cuspidata Siebold & Zucc. Evergreen forests and temperate regions. Native to Japan, Korea, northeast China, and the extreme southeast of Russia.
Mareer Cordia subcordata Lam. Seasonally dry tropical biome, especially coastal forests. Native range extends from Somalia to northern Mozambique, across the western Indian Ocean to the Pacific islands.
Geigertree Cordia sebestena L. Coastal environments, often in dry, broadleaf evergreen forests and shrublands. Native to the American tropics (southern Florida, Bahamas, Central America, Greater Antilles).
Bermudagrass Cynodon dactylon (L.) Pers. Grasslands, savannas, disturbed sites, and cultivated areas. Native to the Old World tropics and subtropics (Europe, Africa, Asia, and Australia).
Yellow Trumpet Handroanthus ochraceus (Cham.) Mattos Wet tropical biome, including forests, savannas, and shrublands. Native to a broad range from Central America to northwestern Argentina and Trinidad.
Mexican Petunia Ruellia simplex C. Wright Marshy/wet areas, wetlands, and disturbed sites with medium to wet soil. Native to southern North America, southern South America, and the Antilles.
Common Boxwood Buxus sempervirens L. Understory of forests, often on soils derived from chalk or limestone. Native to western and southern Europe, northwest Africa, and southwest Asia.
